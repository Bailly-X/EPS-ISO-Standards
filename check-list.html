Plain Checklist
Structure
  • 1. One topic per paragraph ›
    Each paragraph should focus on only one idea. This makes the message easier to follow and reduces confusion. When you mix too many topics in one paragraph, the reader has to work harder to understand. Clear structure improves understanding and makes your writing more professional.
  • 2. Use headings and lists ›
    Headings help the reader know what each part is about. Lists break down information into smaller pieces that are easier to scan. This is especially useful for instructions or steps. It helps busy readers find what they need quickly.
  • 3. Keep sentences short ›
    Short sentences are easier to read and understand. They help the reader stay focused and avoid getting lost in long explanations. Aim for about 15 to 20 words per sentence. This improves clarity and makes your writing more user-friendly.
  • 4. Keep subject & verb close together ›
    The subject is the person or thing doing something. The verb is the action. Keeping them close together makes sentences clearer and easier to understand. When you separate them with extra information, it’s harder for the reader to follow.
  • 7. Use active voice ›
    In active voice, the subject does the action. For example, “We will send your result.” In passive voice, the action happens to the subject, like “The result will be sent.” Active voice is clearer, easier to read, and sounds more human.
Word choice
  • 5. Use everyday words ›
    Choose words that people use in daily life. Avoid formal or technical language when a simpler word will do. Using everyday language helps more people understand your message. It also feels friendlier and more direct.
  • 6. Explain difficult words ›
    Sometimes you must use a difficult word, like a legal or official term. When you do, explain what it means right away. This shows respect for your reader and helps them feel confident. Never assume everyone knows the same vocabulary.
  • 8. Address the reader ›
    Write like you’re speaking directly to the reader. Use “you” to make the message feel personal. This helps the reader feel involved and makes your writing more engaging. It also builds trust and clarity.
  • 9. Omit unneeded words ›
    Say what you mean in as few words as possible. Remove filler, repetition, or formal phrases that don’t add value. This makes your message faster to read and easier to understand. It respects the reader’s time.
  • 10. Proof read your work ›
    After writing, take time to check your text. Look for spelling errors, long sentences, or unclear ideas. Reading your text out loud can help you find awkward phrases. Good proofreading makes your writing clear and professional.
